1. Comparison of Animal and Plant Cells

Plant Cells (Rhoeo discolor)

Structures to observe: Cell wall, central vacuole, chloroplasts, nucleus, cytoplasm streaming

Stain: Iodine to visualize nucleus

Animal Cells (Squamous Epithelia)

Source: Inner cheek lining (collected with toothpick)

Stain: Methylene blue to enhance visibility

Structure: Irregular shape, prominent nucleus

A. Preparation of Plant Cells (Rhoeo discolor)

  1. Peel off the top layer of a leaf from the Rhoeo discolor plant
  2. Prepare a wet mount slide (place specimen in drop of water, add cover slip)
  3. Observe under microscope using medium power (10X)
  4. Describe the motion of cytoplasm surrounding the central vacuole (cytoplasmic streaming)
  5. Add a drop of iodine to stain the nucleus clearly
  6. Make a labelled drawing showing: cell wall, chloroplasts, central vacuole, nucleus

B. Preparation of Animal Cells (Human Squamous Epithelia)

  1. Gently scrape the inside lining of your cheek with a clean toothpick
  2. Mix the cells into a drop of water on a slide
  3. Add a small drop of methylene blue stain
  4. Cover with a cover slip
  5. Observe under microscope using medium power (10X)
  6. Make a labelled drawing showing: cell membrane, cytoplasm, nucleus

2. Measuring Cell Size

Equipment Setup

Your microscope has a graticule fitted into the eyepiece (usually left eyepiece). This consists of a scale with 100 equal divisions. You must calibrate it using a micrometer slide for each objective lens.

Calibration Formula

Actual size of one graticule division = M / 100

Where M = length of the entire graticule measured on the micrometer slide (in mm)

Example: If graticule length M = 0.52 mm on micrometer slide:

Each division = 0.52 mm / 100 = 0.0052 mm = 5.2 μm

Calibration Procedure

  1. Place the micrometer slide under the microscope
  2. Use the medium power objective (10X)
  3. Line up the micrometer scale with the eyepiece graticule
  4. Measure the length of the graticule using the micrometer scale
  5. Record this value as M (in mm)
  6. Calculate size of one graticule division: M / 100

Magnification Calculation

Magnification = Drawing length (mm) / Actual cell length (mm)

Steps:

  1. Measure the length of your drawing in mm
  2. Calculate actual cell length using your mean measurement data
  3. Apply the formula to calculate magnification
  4. Record magnification on your drawing

Questions & Analysis

Critical Thinking Questions

  1. You measured 5 Rhoeo discolor cells. Is there variation in the lengths of these cells? What might explain any variation observed?
  2. Which were larger: the animal or plant cells? Why might this be the case?
  3. What structures are lacking in animal cells compared to what you observed in plant cells?
  4. If you made your drawing of an organism 2x bigger, will this affect:
    • The actual size of the specimen?
    • The magnification of your drawing?
    • If so, how will it affect them?

Microscope Technique Tips

  • Light adjustment: Shut down diaphragm to minimum while maintaining sufficient light
  • Focusing: Use fine focus only at high magnification
  • Measurement: Always measure at the same orientation for consistency
  • Drawing: Draw what you see, not what you expect to see from textbooks
  • Recording: Note all measurements immediately to avoid forgetting

Statistical Analysis

Calculate the following for your data:

  • Mean: Average cell size = (Sum of all measurements) ÷ 5
  • Range: Difference between maximum and minimum values
  • Variation: How much individual cells deviate from the mean

Common Errors to Avoid

  • Forgetting to calibrate the graticule for each objective
  • Using different units (micrometers vs. millimeters)
  • Measuring at different orientations for comparison
  • Not accounting for empty divisions at ends of scale
  • Forgetting to convert graticule divisions to actual measurements
