Practical 8: DNA Extraction from Peas

Isolating the genetic material from plant cells using household chemicals and understanding the structure and function of DNA

What is DNA?

Deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA) is a self-replicating material present in nearly all living organisms as the main constituent of chromosomes. It is the carrier of genetic information and the hereditary material in humans and almost all other organisms.

DNA Structure

Double Helix Structure

DNA consists of two strands that wind around each other like a twisted ladder, forming a double helix.

Base Pairing

Adenine (A) pairs with Thymine (T), and Guanine (G) pairs with Cytosine (C) through hydrogen bonds.

Sugar-Phosphate Backbone

The sides of the DNA ladder are made of alternating sugar and phosphate molecules.

Extraction Chemicals & Their Roles

Blender

Breaks open plant cells by mechanical force

Detergent

Dissolves cell & nuclear membranes, releasing DNA

Salt

Shields negative phosphate groups, helps DNA precipitate

Alcohol

Precipitates DNA (DNA is insoluble in cold alcohol)

Papain/Meat Tenderizer

Enzyme that cuts proteins away from DNA

Extraction Procedure

Step 1: Cell Disruption

Blend split peas with salt and cold water for 30 seconds. This breaks open the plant cells mechanically.

Salt helps preserve DNA structure
Step 2: Filtration

Strain the mixture through cheesecloth to remove large cellular debris, keeping the liquid containing DNA.

Only small molecules pass through
Step 3: Membrane Lysis

Add dishwashing soap to break down cell and nuclear membranes, releasing DNA into solution.

Soap dissolves lipid membranes
Step 4: Cooling

Refrigerate the solution for 10 minutes to slow enzyme activity that might degrade DNA.

Cold temperatures preserve DNA
Step 5: Protein Digestion

Add papain powder (meat tenderizer) to cut histone proteins away from DNA.

Histones package DNA in nucleus
Step 6: DNA Precipitation

Slowly add ice-cold isopropyl alcohol. DNA is insoluble in alcohol and precipitates as white strands.

Alcohol must be cold for best results

Key Questions & Concepts

Why add salt?

Salt (NaCl) provides Na‚Å∫ ions that neutralize the negative charges on DNA phosphate groups. This reduces repulsion between DNA strands, allowing them to come together and precipitate more easily when alcohol is added.

Why use detergent?

Cell membranes are made of phospholipids. Detergent molecules have both hydrophobic and hydrophilic ends that disrupt the membrane structure, breaking open cells and nuclei to release DNA.

Why alcohol precipitation?

DNA is soluble in water but insoluble in alcohol. When alcohol is layered on top of the DNA solution, DNA molecules lose their solubility and precipitate out as visible white strands.

What about RNA?

This extraction actually isolates nucleic acids (both DNA and RNA). The white stringy material contains both, though DNA is the majority. Specialized techniques are needed to separate them.

Important Notes:

  • Use cold alcohol for better DNA precipitation
  • Add alcohol slowly down the side of the test tube to form distinct layers
  • The extracted material contains both DNA and RNA
  • Each pea cell contains about 4.5 billion base pairs of DNA!
