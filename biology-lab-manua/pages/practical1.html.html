Procedure Summary

1. The Transmission Microscope: How to Use

  • Place the slide on the stage, securing it with the stage clip
  • Adjust the eyepieces to fit your eyes - use both eyes for viewing
  • Always start with the low power (4X) objective
  • Focus first using the coarse focus, then refine with fine focus
  • Adjust light using the iris diaphragm - less light often gives sharper image
  • When finished, rotate back to low power before removing slide

2. Focusing on Coloured Silk Threads

You will be provided with a slide containing 3 coloured silk threads arranged in layers. By carefully focusing, determine which colour is on top, in the middle, and underneath.

Technique Tip

To determine thread order: Lower the stage completely, then slowly raise it until the first thread comes into focus. This is the top thread. Continue focusing to find the middle and bottom threads.

3. Focusing on Biological Specimens

Amoeba

Single-celled Protist that moves using pseudopodia. Observe the nucleus and cytoplasmic streaming.

Skeletal Muscle

Striated muscle tissue with long, multinucleated fibers. Note the peripheral nuclei.

Cardiac Muscle

Involuntary striated muscle with centrally located nuclei. Look for intercalated discs.

Biological Drawings Guidelines

Many practicals require you to make drawings of observed specimens. Follow these guidelines:

  • Large: Drawing should fill at least half the page
  • Pencil only: Use a sharp 3H pencil for clean lines
  • No shading: Do not shade your drawings
  • Title: Include a descriptive title above or below
  • Labels: Arrange neatly with ruler-drawn connecting lines that don't cross
  • Accuracy: Represent what you actually see, not textbook images

Pro Tip

When drawing, focus on one structure at a time. Start with the overall shape, then add organelles and details. Use the fine focus knob to see different depths in the specimen.
