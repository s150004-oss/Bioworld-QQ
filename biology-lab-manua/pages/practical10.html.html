Practical 10: Mitosis and Meiosis in Cells

Investigating nuclear division processes: mitosis for growth and repair, meiosis for gamete formation and genetic diversity

Key Concepts

Mitosis: Nuclear division producing two genetically identical daughter cells (2n → 2n). Meiosis: Two nuclear divisions producing four genetically unique haploid cells (2n → n). Chromosomes: Structures carrying genetic information that duplicate and separate during cell division.

Key Differences in Plant vs Animal Mitosis

  • Plant cells: Form cell plate during cytokinesis (no cleavage furrow)
  • Animal cells: Form cleavage furrow during cytokinesis
  • Plant cells: Centrosomes absent; spindle forms from microtubule organizing centers
  • Animal cells: Centrosomes with centrioles present

Genetic Diversity in Meiosis

Crossing Over

Exchange of genetic material between homologous chromosomes during Prophase I

Independent Assortment

Random alignment of homologous pairs at Metaphase I creates 2²³ possible combinations in humans

Random Fertilization

Any sperm can fertilize any egg, further increasing genetic diversity

Result: Each human gamete is genetically unique. With 23 chromosome pairs, meiosis can produce over 8 million (2²³) different chromosome combinations!
